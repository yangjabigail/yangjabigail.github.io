This song was inspired with a battle with thrips, which are tiny, sap-sucking insects that can cause significant damage to plants. As a child, my parents always warned me to wash fruit and avoid eating any produce that could have had pesticides. In high school biology, you also learn that pesticides can have a huge ripple effect across ecosystems. This led me to always feel hesitant to use pesticides, but after researching thrips, I found that usually some sort of intervention would be necessary to save my peace lily.

I ultimately decided to use a natural pesticide called spinosad, which is derived from a soil bacterium and is considered safe for use around pets and children. However, even this "organic" solution can be very harmful to bees and other beneficial insects.

Not wanting to take any chances, I tried to isolate the plant and spray a mixture in the bathroom, away from anything living. However, I couldn't help but feel guilty -- if it were any other creature, this pesticide could be considered cruel.
